A 3-resistor divider on a variable buck converter (typically using a potentiometer \(R_{p}\) and a fixed resistor \(R_{f}\) in series) allows for a wider, more precise tuning range for output voltage \(V_{out}\). The output is calculated by treating the potentiometer as a variable resistor \(R_{p1}\) and \(R_{p2}\) based on the wiper position, where \(V_{out}=V_{ref}\times (1+\frac{R_{top}}{R_{bottom}})\). Calculation Steps for a 3-Resistor Divider (Variable Buck): Identify Components: \(R_{top}\) (top fixed resistor to \(V_{out}\)), \(R_{p}\) (potentiometer), and \(R_{bot}\) (bottom fixed resistor to GND).Determine Target Range: Define \(V_{min}\) and \(V_{max}\) to calculate the needed \(R_{top}\) and \(R_{bot}\) values using the feedback voltage (\(V_{ref}\), usually \(0.8V\) or \(1.25V\)).Formula: The output voltage \(V_{out}\) is determined by the total resistance of the network:\(V_{out}=V_{ref}\times \left(1+\frac{R_{top}}{R_{p}+R_{bot}}\right)\)Wiper Adjustment: Moving the potentiometer \(R_{p}\) changes the effective \(R_{bot}\) value, thus varying \(V_{out}\). Common Configurations: Two Fixed + One Pot: Often used to limit the minimum/maximum voltage range, setting \(R_{top}\) and \(R_{bot}\) to fix boundaries, with \(R_{p}\) in between for fine adjustment.Series/Parallel: \(R_{1}\) is connected to \(V_{out}\), \(R_{2}\) is the pot wiper, \(R_{3}\) is to ground. The formula becomes more complex, often requiring the superposition principle. Example: If \(V_{ref}=0.8V\), \(R_{top}=10k\Omega \), and \(R_{bot}\) varies between \(1k\Omega \) and \(5k\Omega \) using a pot, \(V_{out}\) ranges from \(0.8(1+\frac{10}{5})=2.4V\) to \(0.8(1+\frac{10}{1})=8.8V\).
